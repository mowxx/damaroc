How is this different from regular checkers?

Dama rules used here

Regular pieces move and capture diagonally forward only, in the two forward directions. Kings (crowned pieces) can move and capture across any number of empty squares along a diagonal in all four directions — the "flying king" found in Spanish and North African variants. Captures are mandatory when available, and a single turn can chain up to 3 captures. When multiple capture sequences are possible, only the sequence(s) capturing the most pieces are legal. A piece is crowned upon reaching the farthest row. The game ends when a player has no pieces left or cannot move.
